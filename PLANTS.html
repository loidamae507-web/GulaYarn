Backyard Plants Guide

Explore common edible plants found in Filipino backyards. Learn how to identify them and understand their nutritional value.

Malunggay

Rich in vitamins A, C, and calcium. Helps boost immunity and improve nutrition.

Kangkong

A leafy vegetable rich in iron and fiber. Good for digestion and overall health.

Alugbati (Malabar Spinach)

Contains vitamins and antioxidants that help maintain healthy skin and body functions.

Talbos ng Kamote (Sweet Potato Leaves)

A nutritious leafy vegetable rich in antioxidants, fiber, and vitamins. Helps improve digestion and supports heart health.

Saluyot (Jute)

Known for its slightly sticky texture when cooked. Rich in iron, calcium, and vitamins that help support healthy blood and bones.

Kulitis (Amaranth)

A fast-growing vegetable rich in iron, calcium, and vitamins. A nutritious addition to soups and sautéed dishes.

Labong (Bamboo Shoots)

Rich in fiber and antioxidants, labong supports digestion and adds a crunchy texture to meals.

Ampalaya (Bitter Gourd)

Known for helping regulate blood sugar levels. Rich in vitamins and commonly used in Filipino dishes.

Sitaw (String Beans)

A good source of fiber and vitamins that support digestion and overall body function.

Gabi (Taro)

A versatile plant whose leaves and stalks are edible. Rich in fiber, potassium, and vitamins.

Okra

Contains fiber and antioxidants that support digestion and heart health. Grows well in tropical environments.

These plants are easy to find, grow, affordable, and packed with nutrients—perfect for everyday meals.
