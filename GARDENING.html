Gardening Tips

Growing your own plants is a rewarding and sustainable practice. It not only provides fresh food but also helps reduce expenses and promotes a healthier lifestyle. Here are some simple tips to help you start your backyard garden.

🌱 Basic Tips

  • Water your plants regularly, especially during dry days
  • Ensure your plants receive enough sunlight (at least 4–6 hours daily)
  • Use healthy, nutrient-rich soil
  • Remove weeds to prevent competition for nutrients
  • Use natural fertilizers like compost when possible

🌿 Plant Examples

Malunggay

  • Grows best in full sunlight.
  • Water regularly but avoid overwatering.
  • Can grow directly in soil or large pots.
  • Prune branches often to encourage more leaves.
  • Harvest young leaves for better taste and nutrition.

Kangkong

  • Thrives in moist soil and warm weather.
  • Water daily, especially during hot days.
  • Can grow in containers, plots, or near water sources.
  • Harvest stems regularly to promote regrowth.
  • Avoid overcrowding plants.

Alugbati (Malabar Spinach)

  • Needs a trellis or support for climbing.
  • Prefers warm temperatures and moist soil.
  • Water consistently but ensure drainage.
  • Pick young leaves and shoots frequently.
  • Add compost for healthier growth.

Talbos ng Kamote (Sweet Potato Leaves)

  • Easy to grow from sweet potato cuttings.
  • Prefers loose, well-drained soil.
  • Requires moderate watering.
  • Trim vines regularly to encourage leafy growth.
  • Grows well in sunny areas.

Saluyot (Jutes)

  • Thrives in hot and humid climates.
  • Plant in fertile, well-drained soil.
  • Water consistently for tender leaves.
  • Harvest young leaves before they become tough.
  • Remove weeds regularly.

Kulitis (Amaranth)

  • Fast-growing and easy to maintain.
  • Needs full sunlight and regular watering.
  • Add organic fertilizer every few weeks.
  • Harvest leaves while still young and tender.
  • Protect from insects like aphids.

Labong (Bamboo Shoots)

  • Grows best in moist soil with partial sunlight.
  • Requires enough space because bamboo spreads quickly.
  • Water deeply during dry weather.
  • Harvest shoots while still young and tender.
  • Control spreading roots to manage growth.

Ampalaya (Bitter Gourd)

  • Needs a trellis for climbing vines.
  • Prefers full sunlight and rich soil.
  • Water regularly but avoid waterlogging.
  • Pollination improves fruit production.
  • Harvest fruits while still green and firm.

Sitaw (String Beans)

  • Requires a trellis or poles for support.
  • Plant in sunny areas with loose soil.
  • Water regularly, especially during flowering.
  • Harvest beans young for better flavor.
  • Remove damaged leaves to prevent pests.

Gabi (Taro)

  • Prefers moist or slightly wet soil.
  • Grows well in partial to full sunlight.
  • Water frequently during dry seasons.
  • Use nutrient-rich soil for larger corms and leaves.
  • Wear gloves when handling raw stalks if sensitive to itchiness.

Okra

  • Loves warm weather and full sunlight.
  • Plant in loose, fertile soil.
  • Water deeply 2–3 times a week.
  • Harvest pods while still young and tender.
  • Pick regularly to encourage continuous production.

🌼 Additional Advice

Start with a few plants and gradually expand your garden. Reuse containers like plastic bottles or pots if you have limited space. Gardening is not about perfection—it’s about consistency and care.

With patience and care, your backyard can become a source of fresh and healthy food.
